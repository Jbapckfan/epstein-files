Section 02 — The NPA

The Acosta Plea Deal (2007–2008)

A 60-count federal indictment was drafted, then abandoned in favor of a Non-Prosecution Agreement that immunized not just Epstein but every unnamed co-conspirator. Victims were actively deceived.

Anatomy of the NPA FEDERAL CASE SDFL-2008
  • 60-Count Federal Indictment DRAFTED Then ABANDONED
    Federal prosecutors in the Southern District of Florida prepared a comprehensive 60-count indictment against Epstein. The charges were ready to file. Instead, the entire indictment was abandoned in favor of negotiation. EFTA Vol. 1 — Draft Indictment DI-SDFL-2007; Grand Jury Records
  • Blanket Immunity for Epstein AND "Any Potential Co-Conspirators"
    The NPA's most extraordinary provision: it granted immunity not only to Jeffrey Epstein but explicitly to "any potential co-conspirators" — unnamed, unlimited, and unrestricted. This single clause protected every person who participated in or facilitated the trafficking operation.
    "...any potential co-conspirators of Epstein...shall not be prosecuted federally."
    EFTA Vol. 1 — NPA-2008-001, Paragraph 7
  • Deliberately Structured to Avoid Victim Notification
    The agreement was engineered to circumvent the Crime Victims' Rights Act (CVRA). By classifying the matter in a specific way, prosecutors avoided the legal requirement to notify victims of the plea arrangement. EFTA Vol. 1 — CVRA Violation Analysis; Judge Marra Ruling 2019
  • Victims Told Prosecution Was Ongoing When Deal Was Already Done
    While the NPA was being finalized, prosecutors continued to tell victims and their attorneys that the investigation was active and prosecution was forthcoming. The deal was signed before victims were informed of its existence.
    "We were told the case was still being built. Nobody told us it was already over."
    EFTA Vol. 1 — Victim Impact Statements; Attorney Correspondence 2007–2008
  • OPR Found "Poor Judgment" but Not "Professional Misconduct"
    The DOJ's Office of Professional Responsibility reviewed Acosta's conduct and concluded it constituted "poor judgment" but did not rise to "professional misconduct" — a distinction that shielded Acosta from disciplinary action. EFTA Vol. 1 — OPR Report 2020; DOJ-OPR-2020-Final
  • Acosta: "Leave It Alone" — Epstein "Belonged to Intelligence"
    During the Trump transition vetting process, Acosta reportedly explained his handling of the Epstein case by stating he had been told to "leave it alone" because Epstein "belonged to intelligence."
    "I was told Epstein 'belonged to intelligence' and to leave it alone."
    EFTA Vol. 1 — Transition Vetting Notes; Daily Beast Report 2019-07-08; Vicky Ward Reporting
  • Trump Nominated Acosta as Labor Secretary in 2017
    Despite Acosta's role in the NPA that shielded Epstein and all co-conspirators, and despite the "belonged to intelligence" explanation being known to the transition team, Acosta was nominated and confirmed as Secretary of Labor. He served until July 2019, resigning only after the SDNY arrest of Epstein made continued service politically untenable. EFTA Vol. 1 — Nomination Records; Confirmation Hearing Transcript; Acosta Resignation Letter 2019-07-12
⚠ Key Implication
The NPA's "any potential co-conspirators" clause means that every powerful individual connected to Epstein's operation — regardless of party affiliation — received federal immunity in 2008. This single document is the legal foundation of the cover-up. It was not an oversight; it was the architecture of protection.
